Core idea

APEX score is computed from accelerometer + gyroscope magnitudes. Accelerometer magnitude |a| is proportional to force on the body segment (F = ma). Summing force over time gives impulse, which relates to mechanical work done (Joules). Mechanical work ÷ body efficiency (~25%) = total metabolic energy = calories.

Proposed formula

APEX_raw = (Σ|accel_mag − 9.81| × w_a + Σ|gyro_mag| × w_g) / duration_s

estimated_cal = APEX_raw × body_weight_kg × sport_factor × duration_s

Sport calibration factors (to be tuned)

  • Boxing — MET ≈ 10 → sport_factor ≈ 0.0028
  • Rollerblade — MET ≈ 9 → sport_factor ≈ 0.0025
  • New sports get their own factor when added

Sanity check

APEX 1000 boxing, 70 kg, 3 min → should give ~40–50 cal. Plausible for a hard round.

Challenge implication

Challenge = who burns more calories in the same sport + same duration. Cross-sport comparison stays valid because the physics formula is the same — only the sport_factor differs. Heavier person burns more for same APEX = accounted for if challenges are normalised by weight (APEX_raw, not calories) or kept same-weight. Decision needed: challenge on raw APEX or on calories?
