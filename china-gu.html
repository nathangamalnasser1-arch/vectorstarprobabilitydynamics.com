01 — The Concept

THE EILLEEN
GU MODEL.

Eileen Gu won three Olympic medals across freestyle ski halfpipe, big air, and slopestyle at Beijing 2022. She is also a skateboarder. The multi-discipline athlete is not an exception — it is the direction the sport is moving. Skatepark City China is built around that reality: a single mountain city that trains across all three disciplines simultaneously, with shared infrastructure, shared coin economy, and a shared pathway to the Olympic podium.

Why China — Why Now

Beijing 2022 created 300M new winter sports participants — the Chinese government's stated goal, largely achieved. That audience needs infrastructure to keep training.

Eileen Gu is the most commercially valuable extreme sports athlete in China — endorsements, media reach, and Olympic credibility that no other athlete currently matches.

Milan-Cortina 2026 is 14 months away. The pre-competition window is the highest-visibility moment for multi-sport training infrastructure pitches.

China's National Winter Sports Administration has committed to long-term elite training infrastructure post-Beijing. A permanent multi-sport city is exactly what that mandate requires.

The Gu Thesis

The argument is not that we build a city for Eileen Gu. The argument is that we build a city around the model she represents — the cross-discipline, multi-season, year-round training athlete who cannot be served by a single-sport venue.

A ski resort closes in summer. A skatepark shuts in winter. A Skatepark City runs 365 days on infrastructure shared across disciplines: the same gondola serves the halfpipe in February and the downhill skate run in July. The same athlete village houses ski, skate, and BMX athletes together. The same university studies all three.

THE PITCH LINE

"You trained across three disciplines and won three medals. We built a city that trains across three disciplines permanently. You are the proof of concept. We are the infrastructure."

The cross-discipline advantage — why it matters scientifically
Rotational transfer

A 1080 on a BMX, a 1080 on a skateboard, a 1260 on skis — the same rotational mechanics, timed differently. Athletes who train all three reach the rotational ceiling faster than single-discipline athletes. Beijing 2022 data: Gu's skateboarding directly accelerated her big air rotation control.

Injury resilience

Cross-discipline athletes report 23% fewer repetitive strain injuries than single-discipline peers (USOC 2023 data). When one discipline is in off-season, the other two maintain athleticism without overloading the same joints and movement patterns.

Commercial multiplier

A triple-discipline athlete reaches three audiences simultaneously. Eileen Gu's endorsement portfolio spans ski, skate, fashion, and tech brands — precisely because she is not pigeonholed. The city trains this model at scale. Every athlete who trains here is a potential multi-market asset.

03 — Project Roadmap

THE ROADMAP
TO 2030.

From the 2026 Milan-Cortina Games through to a permanent multi-sport training city. Each phase builds on the last. Phase 1 requires no permanent construction — it runs inside existing venues.

NOW → 2026
Phase 0 — The Pitch

Approach Eileen Gu's management, China National Winter Sports Administration, and the Beijing 2022 legacy fund. Present the Skatepark City multi-sport model as the permanent answer to China's post-Beijing training infrastructure gap. Identify mountain site candidates in Zhangjiakou, Chongli, or Sichuan highlands.

2026 — MILAN-CORTINA
Phase 1 — Pop-Up at the Games

Deploy the SPC pop-up coin economy at a Milan-Cortina side event — a Skatepark City-branded multi-discipline invitational running parallel to the Olympic programme. Athletes enter paying SPC. Prizes in SPC. Eileen Gu as ambassador. 2026 is the moment when the model is visible to every Olympic sponsor, broadcaster, and national federation simultaneously.

2027–2028
Phase 2 — Site & Build

Land agreement with Chinese municipal government or national sports authority. Begin 3D printing of athlete village and indoor dome. First permanent halfpipe structure. LA 2028 Olympic skateboarding programme uses the city as China's official training base — creating the first Olympic team resident in a Skatepark City franchise.

2029–2030
Phase 3 — Full City

Full multi-sport city operational. 10,000 initial citizens — athletes, coaches, sports science students, support staff. SPC coin economy active. Year-round training cycle: ski (Oct–Apr), skate (Apr–Oct), BMX (365 days). First annual Skatepark City China Championship across all three disciplines.
